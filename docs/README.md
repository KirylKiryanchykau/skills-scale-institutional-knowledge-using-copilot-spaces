## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership across all initiatives. The framework spans five key phases: **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Closure & Retrospective**. At its core, OctoAcme's philosophy centers on customer-first prioritization, small incremental deliverables, and data-informed decision-making. This ensures projects remain focused on measurable outcomes while maintaining psychological safety and encouraging continuous learning from each delivery cycle.

Clearly defined cross-functional roles include **Project Managers** (PMs) who coordinate schedules and risks, **Product Managers** (PdMs) who own the vision and backlog, **Developers** who build and test features, and **QA/Testing** who ensure acceptance criteria and reliability. Teams operate with named PM and PdM leads, guaranteeing accountability for both delivery and product quality.

Communication is maintained through daily standups, weekly syncs, and regular stakeholder updates. Risk management is supported by a formal Risk Register, reviewed at weekly meetings and escalated as needed through a tiered response path. Status updates use standardized templates and incident protocols for transparency and effective expectation management.

Quality assurance is embedded in the workflow by requiring small PRs, automated testing and security scans, frequent demos, and meticulous QA before each release. All work is tracked in the project board and includes defined acceptance criteria, estimates, and roles for shared responsibility. Retrospectives after each milestone convert learnings into actionable improvements, reinforcing OctoAcme's commitment to continuous progress.
